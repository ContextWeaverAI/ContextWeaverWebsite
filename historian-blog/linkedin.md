Every smart factory runs a historian. Strip the vocabulary and it's a time-series database — tag, timestamp, value. Append-only, compressed, decades deep.

It nails exactly one kind of question: what was this tag doing at 4pm last Tuesday? Instantly, going back years.

Then you ask why a batch failed. It hands you ten thousand values and zero context. It doesn't know the filler pressure and the glycol temp share a loop. It doesn't know which product was running, or that 4.2 bar is nominal for one SKU and a defect for the next. It stored every number and connected none of them.

A historian is storage, not a model. Perfect recall, zero comprehension — the lesson observability already learned: a metric tells you the system twitched, never why.

Naming the plant was step one (the Unified Namespace, Part 1). Remembering it is step two. Neither one understands it.
